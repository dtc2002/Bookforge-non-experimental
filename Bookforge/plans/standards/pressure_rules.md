# Narrative Pressure Rules

Narrative pressure must remain visible throughout the planning stack.

## Core principle

Every major beat and chapter must either:

- increase pressure
- sustain already high pressure with meaningful motion
- intentionally reduce pressure for recovery, contrast, or regrouping

Unintentional pressure collapse is not allowed.

## Pressure sources

Valid pressure sources include:

- time limit
- pursuit
- secrecy risk
- relationship strain
- moral dilemma
- physical danger
- dwindling resources
- rising cost of failure
- internal fracture
- external escalation

## Planning requirements

Each beat should identify:

- pressure source
- pressure level
- pressure change
- consequence if the protagonist fails

Each chapter should identify:

- pressure at entry
- pressure at exit
- major pressure sources
- what worsens if the chapter objective fails

## Validation rules

The planning stack should fail if:

- too many consecutive beats decrease pressure
- the midpoint does not raise or transform pressure
- the crisis does not represent a peak pressure state
- the climax is not supported by prior escalation
- chapters contain motion without consequence

## Acceptable release

Pressure may decrease temporarily when:

- the protagonist earns a recovery beat
- emotional intimacy deepens stakes
- regrouping reveals a new threat
- a false victory sets up a worse turn

Pressure release must still serve forward motion.
