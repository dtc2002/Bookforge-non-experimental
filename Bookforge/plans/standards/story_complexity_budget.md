# Story Complexity Budget

Bookforge must keep story complexity proportional to the declared length target.

A story that exceeds its complexity budget is likely to become incoherent, underdeveloped, or structurally weak.

## Complexity categories

Track the following:

- major_characters
- supporting_characters
- pov_characters
- active_factions
- major_plotlines
- major_promises
- named_key_locations
- important_objects
- major_secrets
- antagonistic_forces
- lore_expansions

## Default budgets by length

### short_story
- major_characters: 1 to 3
- supporting_characters: 0 to 4
- pov_characters: 1
- active_factions: 0 to 2
- major_plotlines: 1
- major_promises: 3 to 6
- named_key_locations: 1 to 4
- important_objects: 0 to 3
- major_secrets: 0 to 3
- antagonistic_forces: 1 to 2
- lore_expansions: 0 to 2

### novella
- major_characters: 1 to 5
- supporting_characters: 2 to 8
- pov_characters: 1 to 3
- active_factions: 0 to 4
- major_plotlines: 1 to 3
- major_promises: 5 to 10
- named_key_locations: 2 to 8
- important_objects: 1 to 5
- major_secrets: 1 to 5
- antagonistic_forces: 1 to 3
- lore_expansions: 1 to 4

### novel
- major_characters: 1 to 8
- supporting_characters: 4 to 16
- pov_characters: 1 to 5
- active_factions: 1 to 6
- major_plotlines: 1 to 5
- major_promises: 8 to 16
- named_key_locations: 4 to 15
- important_objects: 1 to 8
- major_secrets: 1 to 8
- antagonistic_forces: 1 to 4
- lore_expansions: 1 to 8

## Rules

- Complexity should stay under budget unless explicitly justified.
- Short stories should strongly prefer one dominant plotline.
- Excessive named entities are a warning sign.
- Multiple POVs should be used sparingly, especially in short fiction.
- Mystery, lore, and artifact counts must remain proportional to available word count.
- If complexity exceeds budget, the planner should simplify rather than justify clutter.

## Validation

A planning stack should be flagged if:

- there are too many major moving parts for the selected length
- the cast size is too large to develop meaningfully
- there are too many promises for the story to resolve
- there are too many locations or lore elements for the scope
- too many antagonistic forces dilute the conflict spine

## Preferred simplification strategy

When over budget, reduce in this order:

1. merge redundant characters
2. remove secondary plotlines
3. remove low-value secrets
4. collapse factions
5. reduce named locations
6. remove unnecessary objects/artifacts
7. narrow POV count
