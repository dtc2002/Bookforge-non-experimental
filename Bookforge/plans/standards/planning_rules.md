# Planning Rules

Planning must remain constrained and practical.

## Core rules

- Plan before drafting.
- Do not draft prose during planning stages.
- Do not introduce major named entities unless needed.
- Keep the number of major moving parts small.
- Prefer clarity over novelty.
- Prefer complete arcs over clever fragments.
- Avoid plot hooks that are never intended to resolve.
- Avoid side plots unless they serve the main arc or an explicitly marked sequel hook.

## Audience rules

Target audience is mandatory.

Planning must reflect audience through:

- complexity
- tone
- theme intensity
- vocabulary expectations
- content boundaries
- pacing

## Canon rules

If a universe bible exists:

- do not contradict canon
- do not invent major lore changes
- flag uncertain canon conflicts
- prefer reuse of existing canon constraints over expansion

## Complexity rules

For the first implementation:

- one primary protagonist
- one primary conflict spine
- one dominant plotline
- limited subplots
- limited POV changes

## Structure rules

Each story plan must include:

- clear protagonist goal
- clear opposition
- clear stakes
- act progression
- escalating pressure
- defined ending shape
- setup/payoff tracking

## Promise budget

The planning stack must maintain a limited promise budget.

For early implementation:

- short_story: 3 to 6 major promises
- novella: 5 to 10 major promises
- novel: 8 to 16 major promises

Major promises include:
- mysteries
- prophecies
- hidden identities
- emotional reconciliations
- special objects
- thematic questions requiring explicit payoff

If the planning stack exceeds the promise budget without strong justification, it should be flagged for overcomplexity.

## Prohibited planning behaviors

Do not:

- add random twists with no foreshadowing
- add mystery boxes without intended answers
- add sequel bait unless explicitly requested
- write “and more may happen” filler
- use vague beat descriptions like “things escalate”
- overload the story with too many factions, artifacts, or secrets
