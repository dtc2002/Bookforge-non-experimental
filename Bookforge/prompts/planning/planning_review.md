You are reviewing a Bookforge planning stack.

Inputs:
- Project Brief
- Story Skeleton
- Beat Outline
- Chapter Plan

Your job is to determine whether the planning stack is ready for drafting.

Evaluate all of the following:

1. Brief inheritance
- Does the Story Skeleton faithfully reflect the Project Brief?
- Are genre, target audience, tone, length target, and content boundaries preserved?
- Are required elements preserved?
- Are forbidden elements respected?
- Are originality constraints still respected?
- If the brief specified series intent, ending preference, pacing preference, POV preference, or tense preference, were those preserved or reasonably resolved?

2. Internal consistency
- Do the Story Skeleton, Beat Outline, and Chapter Plan agree with each other?
- Is the protagonist goal consistent across stages?
- Is the opposition force consistent across stages?
- Do the stakes remain coherent?
- Does the ending shape match the actual plan?

3. Narrative structure
- Is there a clear conflict spine?
- Is escalation visible?
- Does the midpoint meaningfully change the story?
- Does the crisis represent severe pressure?
- Is the climax supported by prior beats?
- Does the resolution fit the ending shape?

4. Audience fit
- Does the plan match the target audience in tone, complexity, and likely content?
- Does the pacing fit the declared audience and length?
- Do the likely themes and events fit the audience?

5. Setup / payoff readiness
- Are major setups present?
- Are payoffs identified or clearly planned?
- Are sequel hooks clearly intentional rather than accidental omissions?
- Does the plan avoid mystery-box clutter or excessive unresolved promises?

6. Chapter usefulness
- Does each chapter have a clear purpose?
- Do chapters show progression rather than filler?
- Do entry and exit states change in meaningful ways?
- Are beats reasonably distributed across chapters?

7. Complexity control
- Is the story too complex for the declared length target?
- Is the cast too large for the declared scope?
- Are there too many plot promises, secrets, factions, artifacts, or unresolved threads?
- Is the plan likely to remain draftable without collapsing into confusion?

Rules:
- Be strict.
- Prefer actionable criticism.
- Do not praise weak planning just because it is complete.
- Mark fail if any blocking issue would likely break drafting quality.
- Mark fail if the planning stack materially drifts from the Project Brief.
- Mark fail if the plan is structurally valid but clearly bloated for the selected length target.
- Treat accidental sequel bait, unresolved mandatory promises, or weak climax support as serious issues.

Output must fit the Planning Assessment schema exactly.

STRICT OUTPUT REQUIREMENT

Return ONLY valid JSON.
Do not include explanations.
Do not include markdown.
Do not include text before or after the JSON.
